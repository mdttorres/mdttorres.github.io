I am a **Research Assistant Professor** at the University of Pennsylvania in the
[Machine Biology Group](https://delafuentelab.seas.upenn.edu/), working where **peptide chemistry,
microbiology, and artificial intelligence** meet. My work designs and discovers new antibiotics to
confront the global rise of antimicrobial resistance.

Trained as a synthetic and organic chemist, I pair solid-phase peptide synthesis and
structure–activity-relationship-guided design with **deep learning and generative AI** to mine the
proteomes of living, extinct, and microbial life for *encrypted antimicrobial peptides*. I also
engineer **peptide chemical modifications** for stability and selectivity, and build **low-cost
biosensors** for rapid infectious-disease diagnostics.

<h2 class="mdt-h">research</h2>
<div class="mdt-sub">Four pillars of my lab and collaborations.</div>
<div class="mdt-themes">
<div class="mdt-theme"><div class="n">01</div><h3>Antimicrobial peptides &amp; SAR-guided design</h3><p>How physicochemical and structural descriptors — charge, hydrophobicity, amphipathicity, helicity — govern antimicrobial peptide activity, and how we exploit them to redesign natural toxins (decoralin, polybia-CP, VmCT1, mastoparans) into potent, non-toxic therapeutics.</p><div class="rep">Representative: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-018-0224-2" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Structure-function exploration of Polybia-CP</a></div></div>
<div class="mdt-theme"><div class="n">02</div><h3>AI &amp; deep learning for antibiotic discovery</h3><p>Computational and generative-AI pipelines — pattern recognition, deep learning, diffusion and Bayesian models — that uncover encrypted antibiotics across the human proteome, extinct organisms (molecular de-extinction), the archaeome, and the global microbiome.</p><div class="rep">Representative: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41551-021-00801-1" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mining the human proteome — Nature Biomed. Eng.</a></div></div>
<div class="mdt-theme"><div class="n">03</div><h3>Peptide chemical modification</h3><p>Expanding peptide chemical space with cyclization, disulfide and lactam bridges, non-canonical residues, backbone and terminal modifications, and computationally designed pore-forming scaffolds — tuning stability, selectivity, and mechanism.</p><div class="rep">Representative: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-023-05254-7" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Molecular hybridization for tuning peptide function</a></div></div>
<div class="mdt-theme"><div class="n">04</div><h3>Low-cost biosensors &amp; diagnostics</h3><p>Inexpensive electrochemical and colorimetric biosensors that detect bacterial and viral pathogens in minutes at the point of care — from pencil-graphite SARS-CoV-2 tests to paper-based devices for antibiotic-resistant bacteria.</p><div class="rep">Representative: <a href="https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2106724118" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Minute-scale SARS-CoV-2 detection — PNAS</a></div></div>
</div>
