I am a **Research Assistant Professor** at the University of Pennsylvania in the
[Machine Biology Group](https://delafuentelab.seas.upenn.edu/), working where **peptide chemistry,
microbiology, and artificial intelligence** meet. My work designs and discovers new antibiotics to
confront the global rise of antimicrobial resistance.

Trained as a synthetic and organic chemist, I pair solid-phase peptide synthesis and
structure–activity-relationship-guided design with **deep learning and generative AI** to mine the
proteomes of living, extinct, and microbial life for *encrypted antimicrobial peptides*. I also
engineer **peptide chemical modifications** for stability and selectivity, and build **low-cost
biosensors** for rapid infectious-disease diagnostics.

My research spans organic chemistry, peptide engineering, microbiology, computational biology, and
synthetic biology — from potent antiplasmodial peptide-hormone analogs and reengineered natural
toxins to AI-discovered antibiotics from the human proteome, extinct organisms, the archaeome, and
the global microbiome.
