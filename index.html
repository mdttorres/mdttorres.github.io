I am a Research Assistant Professor at the University of Pennsylvania in the Machine Biology Group, working where peptide chemistry, microbiology, and artificial intelligence meet. My work designs and discovers new antibiotics to confront the global rise of antimicrobial resistance.

Trained as a synthetic and organic chemist, I pair solid-phase peptide synthesis and structure–activity-relationship-guided design with deep learning and generative AI to mine the proteomes of living, extinct, and microbial life for encrypted antimicrobial peptides. I also engineer peptide chemical modifications for stability and selectivity, and build low-cost biosensors for rapid infectious-disease diagnostics.

research

Four pillars of my lab and collaborations.
01

Antimicrobial peptides & SAR-guided design

How physicochemical and structural descriptors — charge, hydrophobicity, amphipathicity, helicity — govern antimicrobial peptide activity, and how we exploit them to redesign natural toxins (decoralin, polybia-CP, VmCT1, mastoparans) into potent, non-toxic therapeutics.

Representative: Structure-function exploration of Polybia-CP
02

AI & deep learning for antibiotic discovery

Computational and generative-AI pipelines — pattern recognition, deep learning, diffusion and Bayesian models — that uncover encrypted antibiotics across the human proteome, extinct organisms (molecular de-extinction), the archaeome, and the global microbiome.

Representative: Mining the human proteome — Nature Biomed. Eng.
03

Peptide chemical modification

Expanding peptide chemical space with cyclization, disulfide and lactam bridges, non-canonical residues, backbone and terminal modifications, and computationally designed pore-forming scaffolds — tuning stability, selectivity, and mechanism.

Representative: Molecular hybridization for tuning peptide function
04

Low-cost biosensors & diagnostics

Inexpensive electrochemical and colorimetric biosensors that detect bacterial and viral pathogens in minutes at the point of care — from pencil-graphite SARS-CoV-2 tests to paper-based devices for antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

Representative: Minute-scale SARS-CoV-2 detection — PNAS
